Bills By Deprivation

Bills incurred in last year, grouped by billing authority, bill reason, and estimated multiple deprivation index of borrower. The multiple deprivation index is an indicator released by the Office for National Statistics for small areas in England, to represent the level of deprivation in that area (1 indicating the most deprived areas, 10 the least). Multiple deprivation is a combination of health, crime, income, education and other measures. This data could be used to see which deprication groups incur the most bills. The relative deprivation of each authority needs to be taken into account before comparing values between authorities.

Column Description
Authority The authority that the bill is assigned to
Reason The reason for the bill e.g. Overdue
Multiple Deprivation decile An estimated index of multiple deprivation for the users. This is a value taken from the user home address and is a deprivation estimation of 1-10. 1 being most deprived, 10 being the least
Number of bills The number of bills that make up this grouping
Total billed The total amount billed in this grouping
